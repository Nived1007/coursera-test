GREATEST FOOTBALLERS OF ALL TME

LIONEL MESSI

Lionel Messi is dubbed the Goal Machine for a reason, and he’s living up to the title. In La Liga, Messi holds the record for the most goals with 423 goals. In a season, Messi also holds the record for the most goals, having scored 50 goals in a season. In Europe’s top club competition, the UEFA Champions League, Messi has the most hat-trick scored by a player with 8 hat-tricks. In the Copa America, he has scored 13 goals, the most for any South American footballer. Aside goals, Messi is the current footballer in the Spanish league, the La Liga, with the most assists, with 173 assists. He is also the all-time top goalscorer for the Argentinian national football team, having scored over 68 goals. Messi has scored a total of 676 goals, in 831 appearances for his club as well as his country.

CRISTIANO RONALDO

In October 2019, CR7, made headlines when he reached 700 career goals, a feat which made him the 6th highest goalscorer of all time. Majority of the goals scored by Ronaldo has been for his club, he has scored 607 goals for the various clubs he has played in. While at Laliga side, Real Madrid, Ronaldo scored 450 goals. Under the guidance of a renowned coach, Alex Ferguson at EPL side, Manchester United, Ronaldo scored 119 goals. He is also the second all-time highest goal scorer for the national team and the all-time highest goalscorer for the Portuguese national side, with 95 goals. In the UEFA Champions League, Europe’s top club tournament, Cristiano Ronaldo has scored 127 goals, making him all-time tournament’s top scorer. Ronaldo also has created other records, which has not been broken by any other player.

NEYMAR JR

Neymar came into prominence at an early age at Santos, where he made his professional debut aged 17. He helped the club win two successive Campeonato Paulista championships, a Copa do Brasil, and the 2011 Copa Libertadores, with the latter being Santos' first since 1963. Neymar was twice named the South American Footballer of the Year, in 2011 and 2012, and soon relocated to Europe to join Barcelona. As part of Barça's attacking trio with Lionel Messi and Luis Suárez, he won the continental treble of La Liga, the Copa del Rey, and the UEFA Champions League, and finished third for the FIFA Ballon d'Or in 2015 for his performances. He was a key player in Brazil's victories at the 2011 South American Youth Championshipwhere he finished as leading goalscorer, and the 2013 FIFA Confederations Cup, where he won the Golden Ball as player of the tournament
